## SEGMENT 1 — Stephen Lewis Dies at 88; Son Avi Takes NDP Helm

[HOST] We begin with a story that is equal parts heartbreaking and historic. Stephen Lewis — former Ontario NDP leader, Canadian ambassador to the United Nations, UNICEF deputy director, and one of the most powerful orators this country has ever produced — passed away peacefully in hospice care in Toronto early yesterday morning. He was eighty-eight. He had been living with inoperable abdominal cancer since 2018. (Source: CBC News, March 31, 2026)

[HOST] His death came just two days after his son, Avi Lewis, was elected leader of the federal NDP in a decisive first-ballot victory, capturing fifty-six percent of the vote. Avi now inherits a party with just six MPs, about thirteen million dollars in debt, and polling in the single digits — but he struck a defiant tone, declaring quote, "the NDP comeback starts now." (Source: Global News, March 29, 2026)

[HOST] Not everyone in the NDP family is celebrating, though. Alberta NDP Leader Naheed Nenshi said Lewis's election is quote "not in the interests of Alberta," and Saskatchewan's Carla Beck has refused to meet with him until he walks back his stance on fossil fuels. The internal fault lines are real. (Source: Global News, March 30, 2026)

## SEGMENT 2 — Alberta Separatists Claim Enough Signatures for Referendum

[HOST] Speaking of Alberta — the organizers behind the "Stay Free Alberta" independence petition say they've gathered the roughly one hundred and seventy-eight thousand signatures needed to force a separation question onto October's provincial referendum ballot. Elections Alberta says it can't confirm that count until the petition is formally submitted by the May second deadline. (Source: CBC News / National Observer, April 1, 2026)

[HOST] Quick context: polling still shows about two-thirds of Albertans oppose leaving Canada. A counter-petition called "Forever Canadian" has already gathered over four hundred and thirty-eight thousand signatures. And First Nations groups have launched a court challenge arguing the whole exercise violates treaty rights and Section 35 of the Constitution. Premier Smith says she'll put any qualifying petition on the ballot — but maintains she personally supports quote "a sovereign Alberta within a united Canada." Make of that what you will. (Source: CTV News / Policy Options, March–April 2026)

## SEGMENT 3 — Toronto Lands $3-Billion Waterfront LRT Deal

[HOST] Big infrastructure news out of Toronto: the federal, provincial, and city governments have each committed one billion dollars — three billion total — to build the Waterfront East LRT. The three-point-eight-kilometre line will run from Union Station down to the Port Lands and the new Ookwemin Minising island. Mayor Olivia Chow called it the quote "missing piece" of Toronto's waterfront. Prime Minister Carney says it'll unlock seventy-five thousand new homes and over a hundred thousand jobs. Trains are expected to be running by the early 2030s. (Source: CBC News / CP24, March 30, 2026)

## SEGMENT 4 — Economy Check: GDP, Rates & Trade

[HOST] On the economic front — Statistics Canada reports the economy grew a modest zero-point-one percent in January, with a preliminary estimate of zero-point-two percent for February. BMO's Doug Porter called the start of 2026 quote "surprisingly OK." The Bank of Canada held its policy rate at two-point-two-five percent in March, and markets are pricing in a ninety-four percent chance of another hold at the April decision. Unemployment ticked down to six-point-five percent, though that's partly because fewer people are looking for work. (Source: BNN Bloomberg / Statistics Canada, March 31, 2026)

[HOST] On trade, sectoral tariffs on steel, aluminum, autos, and lumber continue to bite, and the formal CUSMA review is looming — with Canada reportedly on the sidelines while Mexico does the talking. (Source: Globe and Mail, 2026)
