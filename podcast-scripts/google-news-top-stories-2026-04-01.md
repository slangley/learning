## SEGMENT 1 — Top News & Releases

[HOST] Let's start with the story that has the whole world watching the skies tonight. NASA's Artemis II mission is set to launch this evening at 6:24 PM Eastern from Kennedy Space Center in Florida. The countdown clock is ticking, and the weather forecast is sitting at 80 percent "Go." This is the first crewed mission beyond low Earth orbit since Apollo 17 in 1972 — over fifty years ago. Four astronauts are strapped into the Orion spacecraft atop the Space Launch System, the most powerful operational rocket in the world. Commander Reid Wiseman, pilot Victor Glover, and mission specialists Christina Koch and Jeremy Hansen of the Canadian Space Agency will loop around the far side of the Moon, passing within about 4,700 miles of the lunar surface before splashing down in the Pacific on April 10th. And here's what makes this crew historic: Glover will become the first person of color, Koch the first woman, and Hansen the first non-American to travel beyond low Earth orbit. It's a ten-day mission, and if all goes well, it paves the way for Artemis III — the mission that will actually land astronauts on the Moon. This one's been delayed a few times — a hydrogen leak in February, then a helium flow issue — but NASA says they are go for launch. (Source: NASA, March 31, 2026; Fox Weather, April 1, 2026)

[HOST] Now, to the conflict dominating global headlines. The Iran-US war and the Strait of Hormuz crisis continue to escalate. A quick recap for anyone just tuning in: since late February, the United States and Israel have conducted joint military strikes on Iran following the killing of Iran's supreme leader. Iran retaliated with missiles and drones and effectively shut down the Strait of Hormuz — the narrow waterway through which roughly twenty percent of the world's oil supply flows. The Pentagon launched "Operation Epic Fury" on March 19th to forcibly reopen the strait, and as of today, we are on day thirty of that operation. White House Press Secretary Karoline Leavitt said yesterday that reopening the strait is, quote, "not a core objective" of winning the war — a shift in tone from the administration. Meanwhile, Iran is demanding sovereignty over the strait and proposing tolls of two million dollars per tanker, which could generate over 800 million dollars a month. The economic fallout is real: US gas prices have crossed four dollars a gallon for the first time since 2022, and Dubai crude hit a record 166 dollars a barrel. President Trump is expected to deliver an Address to the Nation tomorrow night with, quote, "an important update on Iran." We'll be watching closely. (Source: Time, March 30; NPR, March 26; CNBC, March 28, 2026)
